Change your future by revising the past

Ever thought about how you can change your future by revising your past? It all sounds too quantum-physics-B.S, I know. But how do you even define the past? Something that has already happened. We, however, tend to relive those past experiences in the present moment. And what happens when you re-live the past by playing it over and over again in your head? Your past then becomes your present, which in turn becomes the future. Neville Goddard, in one of his books, talked about the Revision method whereby before you go to sleep, in a drowsy state, which is the theta state, you replay the scenes in your head that you didn’t like that happened throughout the day very vividly, and alter it to something positive. Someone said something you didn’t like? Change it to something that you did like. Someone pissed you off? Change it to you being happy and blissful.

How Stress Is Killing You and How You Can Cope With It

Whether it be work related problems or finances turmoil, most of us get stressed out every now and then. But what kind of impact does taking stress cause in terms of our health? Every time we stress, we raise our cortisol levels, also known as, “the stress hormone”, which causes inflammation in our body. In an article published by Science Daily, it has been shown that excessive cortisol levels can have irreversible impact on our health and body, resulting in ineffectual brain capacity in individuals. According to the American Institute Of Stress, those who have chronic stress in their lives tend to have higher chances of getting stroke, heart diseases and overall declination of physical health. The review paper done by Montreal de University found out that when a pregnant woman takes psychological stress, also known as prenatal stress, the child after birth has lower birthweight and lower size as well as disturbances in both neurological and cognitive aspects.

Love the Process More than The End-Result

Most of us refrain from starting something we truly desire due to fear. Imagine setting out to climb a mountain with your focus solely on reaching the summit. In this mindset, every path leading to the mountain feels burdensome, as your mind is fixated on the destination. The journey becomes a source of resentment, overshadowed by the obsession to reach the top. When you finally do reach the summit after enduring struggles, the joy you anticipated is often lackluster. Now, consider a different scenario where you enjoy every moment of the journey towards the peak. You find pleasure in walking for hours, embracing the cold, and conquering uphill terrain, knowing each step leads you closer to your goal. If you aim to shed 20 kilograms from your body, focusing solely on the end result might make the journey seem endless. However, by cherishing the process and celebrating your daily progress, you'll effortlessly reach your goal. Looking back, you'll find it exhilarating to see how far you've come, not even realizing when or how you achieved your target because you were wholeheartedly engrossed in the journey itself.
